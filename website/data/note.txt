 1. The "Generative Flow Field" (Technical & Organic)
  A high-performance <canvas>-based background using Perlin Noise to create thousands of tiny, moving particles that leave faint trails.
   * The Impression: It looks like a flowing digital river or wind. When the user moves their mouse, the particles "swerve" or "flow" around the cursor, creating a physical sense of presence.
   * Why it's Impressive: It demonstrates an understanding of math and physics (vectors/noise) and efficient rendering (requestAnimationFrame).
   * Tech: Vanilla Canvas API or framer-motion for state-to-canvas mapping.


  2. "Floating 3D Geometry" (Architectural & High-End)
  A minimal, wireframe 3D object (like a complex torus knot or a geodesic sphere) that slowly rotates in a fixed background position.
   * The Impression: Using @react-three/fiber, the object can react to scroll depth. As the user scrolls down to "Projects," the object could unfold, change shape, or zoom in.
   * Why it's Impressive: It signals you are comfortable with WebGL and 3D space, which is a massive differentiator for front-end developers in 2026.
   * Tech: @react-three/fiber, @react-three/drei, and r3f-perf for monitoring.


  3. "The Kinetic Grid" (Minimalist & Precision)
  A faint, fixed background grid (10px x 10px) where every intersection is a tiny dot.
   * The Impression: When the mouse moves, the dots within a 100px radius don't just light up—they physically displace (push away) from the cursor and snap back with a bouncy, elastic animation.
   * Why it's Impressive: It feels like a high-end hardware interface. It’s tactile and responsive, making the website feel like a physical tool rather than a static page.
   * Tech: Framer Motion useSpring and usePointerWheel hooks for high-frequency coordinate updates.


  4. "The Bento-Scanner" (Narrative & Focused)
  A "scanning" beam of light that periodically sweeps horizontally or vertically across the page.
   * The Impression: As the light beam passes over your project cards, it briefly "reveals" a more detailed version of the card (e.g., shifting from wireframe to full color, or revealing a hidden tech stack      
     badge).
   * Why it's Impressive: It creates a "revelation" effect. It forces the recruiter to focus on specific elements of your work as the "scan" highlights them.
   * Tech: CSS Masking (mask-image) combined with a moving linear-gradient and Framer Motion for synchronization.


  My Professional Choice: If you want "Instant Wow" for 2026, go with Concept 2 (3D Geometry). A subtle, high-frame-rate 3D element that reacts to scroll creates a sense of depth that 2D sites simply cannot      
  match. It feels "expensive" and professional.
